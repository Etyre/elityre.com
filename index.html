About

This is the personal website of Eli Tyre.

I am an indpendent scholar, I'm currently based in Berkeley, California. I work on a diversity of projects, but the following is a decent overview of my major areas of interest.

Long-termist Strategy

My main, and most important, work is my research and writing on global strategy, trying to get a grasp on what is happening in the world that matters in the long run, and what paths exist for humanity surviving the past 2200 and going on to create a safe, sane, civilization.

Discourse Methods

Since 2016, I've spent a consistent chunk of effort studying and developing effective discourse methods, especially methods for espistemically resolving thorny disagreements and bridging deep differences in worldviews.

Applied Psycholgy

Over the years, I've explored and experimented all kinds of personal development interventions, especially strategies for efficient learning and maintaining high throughput, and trying to tease out the underlying psychological mechanisms of the things that work.

Efficacy Engineering is my applied psychology blog and wiki, where I host outlines of my current systems, document some of my learning / meta-learning projects in real time, and generally iterate to a more true and useful ontology of these domains.

Other Stuff

Musings and Rough Drafts is where I post my even-less-polished-than-usual, unrigorous and stream of conciousness thinking. Mostly it seems to be about sociological and cultural trends.

I also sometimes write threads on twitter, where my handle is @EpistemicHope. I also archive virtually all my threads on threadreader, so you can read them there without needing to frequent a social media site. My twitter content is mostly my posing questions and making posits about what I'm currently reading.
